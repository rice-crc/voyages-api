South Sea House London 18 Jany 1727/8
Gent
Having received Your Letter of the 30th Sepr. last
acquainting us of your being forced away from Cartagena, & Arrival
in Jamaica, & considerd the present uncertain Situation of the puh
Affairs as to peace or Was; We think proper to direct You to take
the first Opportunity of embarking for London And as We are
desirous of having Copys of all Such Books papers & Accot as
have not already been sent home, You are imediately on Recei
hereof to sett about making Copys of them, And if you can get
them done before you meet with a passage futher, You are to brin
them with you, taking Care they be attested as true Copys. But
any of them should not be firnwhed by that Time, You will leave sich
to the Care of Mr Tatter to get done, & to forward to Us; You aeald
to leave wth him All originals, making a List of them & taking
Mr Pratters Receipts for the same one of wh You are to send us
Vesel Ver rema
Yor Loving Friends
